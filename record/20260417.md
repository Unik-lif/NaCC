### Why `PRIVATE_FILE_COW` Can Stay Bitmap-Off While Trap Counts Stay High

This is the most important interpretation point.

What `PRIVATE_FILE_COW set=0` means:

- file-backed private/COW leaf pages were **not** promoted into `PRIVATE_DATA` just because of their region class

What high `PRIVATE_DATA` trap counts mean:

- the kernel/runtime repeatedly touched **other** pages that **were** private, mainly anonymous private buffers and temporary storage

So these two facts are compatible:

- file/COW pages stay non-private
- total `PRIVATE_DATA` traps are still high

Why:

- file/pipeline workloads do not spend their entire time touching file-backed leaves directly
- they also drive repeated accesses to:
  - user buffers
  - shell stack
  - temporary heap data
  - pipe-related data movement
  - usercopy source/destination pages

Those pages are typically anonymous private pages, and those are exactly the pages that the current policy does turn into `PRIVATE_DATA`.

So the right mental model is:

- high trap count here does **not** mean "many file-COW pages became private"
- it means "a smaller number of anonymous private pages are being hit very frequently by copy-heavy paths"

### Operational Implication

The rerun supports the following planning interpretation:

- keep the conservative selective-private baseline
- do not widen `PRIVATE_FILE_COW` based on this evidence
- if optimization work starts, it should begin from the observed copy-heavy / syscall-buffer-heavy profile
- an explicit shared-buffer / agent-managed window route is a more evidence-aligned next experiment than broader private coverage
